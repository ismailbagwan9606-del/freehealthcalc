How Is the Due Date Calculated?

The most common method is Naegele's Rule: add 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). This assumes a 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14.

If your cycle length is different from 28 days, the calculator adjusts accordingly. If you know your conception date, it adds 266 days (38 weeks) from that date.

Remember: This is an estimate. Ultrasound dating, especially in the first trimester, is the most accurate way to confirm your due date.
