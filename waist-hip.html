Why Waist-to-Hip Ratio Matters

The waist-to-hip ratio (WHR) is a measurement of the proportion of fat stored around the waist compared to the hips. It's a better predictor of cardiovascular disease risk than BMI alone, because it reflects where fat is stored — abdominal fat is more dangerous than hip/thigh fat.

The World Health Organization (WHO) considers WHR to be a strong indicator of health risk, particularly for metabolic syndrome, type 2 diabetes, and cardiovascular disease.
